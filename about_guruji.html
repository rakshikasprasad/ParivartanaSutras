A Tribute to Seshan Guruji (Prof. P.A.Seshan) – My Beloved Mentor, a Master Beyond Compare

There are some souls who walk this earth once in a generation. Rare jewels who embody an ocean of knowledge, wisdom, and boundless love.

Seshan Guruji (Prof. P.A. Seshan) was one such being. He was an ocean of knowledge, with deep, piercing yogic eyes, eyes that seemed to hold the Vedas, the Upanishads, and the unspoken wisdom of the universe. Every word he uttered felt like an echo from the ancient Rishis, yet every glance he gave felt like a father’s tender blessing. His words carried the weight of ancient truths, yet they were so accessible, so practical, and so deeply infused with love.

Seshan Guruji was a master of masters, a rare blend of academic brilliance, spiritual depth, and practical wisdom. A University Gold Medalist in English Literature, an All-India First Rank Holder in the 1955 Civil Services (IAAS), and a visionary leader in both public and private sectors, he held senior positions like Executive Director of BHEL, Vice-President of Bhilwara Group, and Executive Director of Keltron.

He also helped establish the Management Development Institute (MDI) in Gurgaon and taught leadership, strategy, and finance as a Professor of Management.

Even at the pinnacle of his corporate career, he never saw a divide between the boardroom and spiritual practice. He was the rarest of minds who could traverse seamlessly from the boardrooms of Fortune 500 companies to the sanctums of esoteric traditions like Sri Vidya. He lived the synthesis of the worldly and the spiritual, a rare integration that few could ever achieve.

As a seeker, his thirst for knowledge was insatiable. He learnt Hatha Yoga from stalwarts like T. Krishnamacharya, Kundalini Yoga from Swami Vethathiri Maharishi, and was honored as an “Arul Nidhi” for his mastery.

He travelled to Japan to learn Soto Zen from Master Haku-un Enomiya-Lassalle, becoming one of the few Indian teachers of authentic Japanese Zen.

He was a Vedic scholar, a devotee of Hanuman, and a Sri Vidya master initiated into the sacred tradition by Sri Amritananda Natha Saraswati of Devipuram, who gave him the monastic name Amritapadananda Natha.

He was a walking library, a master of the Vedas, Upanishads, and mantras. I still remember how he would sit surrounded by thousands of books and tell you exactly where to find a particular line on a particular page.

His memory was extraordinary, his intellect razor-sharp. And yet, despite his towering intellect, he was the most humble and compassionate teacher, always ready to guide, never withholding his wisdom.

His hands would lovingly pass on the 64 Parivarthana Sutras, printed on simple A4 papers, telling us, “This is all you need. If you master this, your life will be transformed.” And he was right.

These 64 Parivarthana Sutras are a treasure, a lifetime of knowledge distilled into practical, transformative principles.

As a mental health professional, I can say with conviction that these teachings on mind-body alignment and inner discipline have given me insights far beyond any textbook or clinical training. They form the framework for ultimate well-being

This site is my humble attempt to share the 64 Parivarthana Sutras that Guruji so generously imparted, a treasure that must not be lost to time.

Seshan Guruji’s grace was oceanic. He embodied the essence of Zen stillness, Sri Vidya devotion, and yogic power.

His workshops on stress management, prosperity, and meditation guided over 10,000 professionals, helping them navigate life’s challenges with clarity and composure.

He taught that every single cell of the body should resonate with the mantra when you pray, a teaching that still echoes within me. His words, “99% is failure; aim for 100% alignment,” were not just guidance but a call to inner mastery.

There are no words deep enough to capture the privilege of learning from such a master.My eyes well up when I think of him. His presence was love itself, his mind a vast universe, his heart a fountain of compassion.

It is a sacred responsibility and a blessing to carry forward his vision, especially the 64 Parivarthana Sutras, as a foundational framework for mental health and inner transformation.

His life was a living bridge between corporate leadership and yogic mastery, between strategic thinking and spiritual awakening.

To me, he was not just a mentor. He was a light, a force of nature, a soul who embodied the infinite.

I will forever cherish the grace of his gaze, the warmth of his words, and the silent power of his presence. Seshan Guruji, you live on in every breath, every prayer, every act of service I offer to this world.

May your blessings continue to inspire and guide all who walk the path you illuminated, for generations to come.

With a heart full of Gratitude - Indrani
